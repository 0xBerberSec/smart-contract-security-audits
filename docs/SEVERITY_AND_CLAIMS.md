# Severity and Claims

[Back to index](./INDEX.md)

This repository separates validated findings, QA reports, and independent research. A claim is only presented as validated when an external platform or contest has validated it.

## Categories

## Validated Findings

Findings in this category have an external validation source, such as a public contest submission with an accepted severity.

Example:

```text
LayerZero Stellar Endpoint
Severity: Low
Status: Validated
Platform: Code4rena
```

## QA Research

QA research covers issues that improve safety, maintainability, invariants, or defensive guarantees, but do not demonstrate a confirmed economic exploit.

These reports should not be described as validated vulnerabilities unless the platform explicitly validated them as such.

## Independent Security Research

Independent research may demonstrate a real issue or security-relevant behavior, but it is not presented as an official contest result.

These reports must avoid language that implies external validation, accepted severity, or a confirmed bounty outcome.

## Claim Discipline

Public reports should clearly distinguish:

- what the PoC demonstrates;
- what assumptions are required;
- what impact is confirmed;
- what impact is not claimed.

## Terms Avoided Without Proof

The following claims are avoided unless directly supported by strong evidence:

- maximum-severity language;
- broad control compromise;
- full protocol asset loss;
- guaranteed exploitability;
- externally accepted severity;
- externally accepted vulnerability status.

## Publication Rule

If a report requires speculative wording to appear strong, it should not be published.
